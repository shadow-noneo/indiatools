Common Questions

Are generic medicines safe? +
Yes. Generic medicines contain the exact same active ingredient (salt) in the same dose as branded medicines. They are tested and approved by CDSCO (Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation) in India. The only difference is the brand name and price.
Why are generic medicines so much cheaper? +
Branded medicine companies spend crores on research, marketing, and doctor promotions — costs that are passed to you. Generic manufacturers skip all of that. They only manufacture the medicine, which is why they can sell at 70-90% less.
What is Jan Aushadhi? +
Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Kendra (PMBJK) is a Government of India initiative to provide quality generic medicines at affordable prices. There are 10,000+ Jan Aushadhi stores across India where you can get medicines at the lowest possible prices.
Can I switch from branded to generic without asking my doctor? +
For most common medicines like paracetamol, antacids, and vitamins — yes, it is generally safe. However for critical medicines like blood pressure, diabetes, thyroid, or psychiatric medicines, always consult your doctor before switching. They can confirm the equivalent dose.
Where can I buy generic medicines? +
1) Jan Aushadhi stores (cheapest) — find nearest at janaushadhi.gov.in 2) Any local pharmacy — just ask for the salt name instead of brand name 3) Online pharmacies like 1mg, Netmeds, PharmEasy also stock generic options.
