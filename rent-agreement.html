Frequently Asked Questions

Is a rent agreement mandatory in India? ▼
While not strictly mandatory by law for short-term tenancies, a rent agreement is highly recommended for both landlord and tenant. It protects both parties legally. For agreements above 12 months, registration under the Registration Act 1908 is mandatory.
Do I need to get the agreement notarized? ▼
Notarization is optional for 11-month agreements but adds legal weight. Registration (at Sub-Registrar office) is required for agreements above 1 year. For 11-month agreements on stamp paper with signatures, the agreement is generally admissible as evidence in court.
What value stamp paper should I use? ▼
For 11-month rent agreements: ₹100 stamp paper is generally sufficient in most states. Maharashtra requires ₹500 stamp paper. Karnataka requires stamp duty of 0.5% of annual rent + deposit. Check your state's stamp duty rules for the exact amount.
Can a landlord keep the security deposit? ▼
The landlord can deduct from the security deposit for unpaid rent, damage to property beyond normal wear and tear, or unpaid utility bills. However, they must refund the remaining amount within a reasonable time (usually 30-60 days) after the tenant vacates. Withholding deposit without valid reason can be legally challenged.
