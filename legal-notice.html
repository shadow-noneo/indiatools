Frequently Asked Questions

Is a self-drafted legal notice valid? ▼
Yes. In India, there is no law requiring a legal notice to be drafted by a lawyer. A notice drafted by you personally or using this tool is completely valid. However, for cases involving large amounts or criminal matters, a lawyer-drafted notice adds more weight.
What if the person ignores the notice? ▼
If the recipient ignores the notice within the given time period, you can file a case in the appropriate forum — Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (for consumer matters), Civil Court, or approach the Police for criminal matters. The notice itself becomes evidence in court.
How much does it cost to send a legal notice? ▼
Using this tool, generating the notice is free. Sending via registered post costs ₹30-50. If you hire a lawyer to draft and send it, expect to pay ₹1,000 to ₹5,000 depending on the complexity. This tool saves you that cost entirely.
Can I send this notice via email? ▼
Yes, you can send it via email as well. However, registered post is legally stronger as it provides undeniable proof of delivery. Ideally, send it both ways — registered post AND email — and keep copies of both.
