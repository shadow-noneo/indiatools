Frequently Asked Questions

Can I really fight a challan in India? ▼
Yes! Under the Motor Vehicles Act 1988 (amended 2019), every vehicle owner has the right to dispute a challan they believe is incorrect. You can approach the traffic police office, submit a written dispute, or contest it before a Judicial Magistrate. Many challans — especially camera-based e-challans — contain errors.
What if my vehicle number was misread by the camera? ▼
This is very common with ANPR (Automatic Number Plate Recognition) cameras, especially with similar-looking numbers (0 vs O, 1 vs I, etc.). You can dispute by providing your vehicle's RC (Registration Certificate) and pointing out the discrepancy. Include photos if possible.
Where do I submit this dispute letter? ▼
You can submit it physically at your nearest traffic police station or at the office of the Traffic Commissioner. For e-challans, you can also dispute online at echallan.parivahan.gov.in. Always keep a copy with acknowledgement.
What happens if I don't pay or dispute a challan? ▼
Unpaid challans can result in your vehicle registration being blocked at RTO, difficulties in renewing insurance, or action during traffic checks. In some states, repeated unpaid challans may lead to suspension of driving licence. It's best to either pay or formally dispute within 60 days.
