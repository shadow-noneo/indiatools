Everything You Need to Know

About affidavits and how to use them in India

📜
What is an Affidavit?
An affidavit is a written sworn statement of facts, voluntarily made under oath. It is legally binding and used as evidence in courts, government offices, and official procedures.
🖨️
How to Make It Valid
1) Print on ₹100 non-judicial stamp paper 2) Sign in front of a Notary Public or First Class Magistrate 3) Get the notary seal and signature 4) It is now legally valid across India.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this affidavit legally valid in India? +
Yes, once you print it on ₹100 stamp paper and get it notarised by a Notary Public or First Class Magistrate, it is legally valid across all of India. The format used here follows the standard legal format accepted by courts and government offices.
What stamp paper value should I use? +
For most affidavits in India, ₹100 non-judicial stamp paper is sufficient. Some states like Maharashtra may require higher value stamp paper for specific purposes. When in doubt, ₹100 stamp paper is accepted for general affidavits in all states.
Where can I get it notarised? +
You can get an affidavit notarised at: 1) Any Notary Public office (search "notary near me") 2) District Court premises — notaries are always available outside court buildings 3) Some authorised stamp vendors also provide notary services. Cost is usually ₹50–200.
Can I use this for name change after marriage? +
Yes! Select "Name Change Affidavit" from the options above. After marriage, you need: 1) This affidavit stating your old and new name 2) Marriage certificate 3) Newspaper publication (optional but recommended) 4) Gazette notification (for central government employees). The affidavit alone is sufficient for most banks, schools, and private organisations.
Do I need a lawyer to make an affidavit? +
No! You do not need a lawyer for standard affidavits. You can draft it yourself (which our tool does for you), print on stamp paper, and get it notarised directly. A lawyer is only needed for complex legal matters involving property disputes, court cases, or special circumstances.
Which affidavit do I need for passport address change? +
For passport address change, use the "Address Proof Affidavit" option. The Passport Seva Kendra accepts a notarised address affidavit as valid address proof. You will also need one additional supporting document like a utility bill or bank statement showing the new address.
