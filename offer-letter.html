Frequently Asked Questions

Is an offer letter legally binding in India? ▼
An offer letter is generally considered a legally binding document once accepted by the candidate, as it constitutes a basic contract under the Indian Contract Act, 1872. However, it is advisable to follow it up with a detailed Employment Agreement that covers all terms including confidentiality, IP ownership, and termination clauses.
What is CTC vs take-home salary? ▼
CTC (Cost to Company) is the total amount a company spends on an employee annually, including salary, PF contributions, gratuity, insurance premiums, etc. Take-home (in-hand) salary is what the employee actually receives after all deductions like PF, Professional Tax, TDS. Typically take-home is 70-80% of CTC.
Can I use this for internship offer letters too? ▼
Yes! Select "Internship" under Employment Type. The generated letter will be appropriately worded for internship positions. You can also set the probation period to "No probation" for internships.
What documents should I ask the candidate to bring on joining? ▼
Standard documents include: Educational certificates (10th, 12th, Graduation), Previous employment offer letters and relieving letters, Last 3 months salary slips, PAN card, Aadhaar card, Passport size photographs, Bank account details, and any professional certifications.
