Frequently Asked Questions

Can I really dispute an insurance rejection? ▼
Yes. Under IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India) guidelines, every policyholder has the right to formally dispute a claim rejection. The insurer is legally required to respond within 15 days of receiving your dispute letter.
What if they reject my dispute too? ▼
You can escalate to the Insurance Ombudsman — a free, independent government body that resolves insurance complaints. You can also approach the consumer forum or IRDAI directly. Most cases are resolved at the grievance officer level itself.
Is this letter legally valid? ▼
This letter is based on standard legal dispute language used in India. However, it is not a substitute for professional legal advice. For large claims above ₹5 lakh, we recommend consulting a lawyer or insurance advisor.
How should I send this letter? ▼
Send via registered post (keep the acknowledgement receipt) AND email to the grievance officer's email address. This creates a legal paper trail. The insurer's grievance email is usually on their website under "Contact Us".
Is my data safe? ▼
100%. This tool runs entirely in your browser. No data is sent to any server. We don't store, log, or see any of the information you enter. The letter is generated locally on your device.
