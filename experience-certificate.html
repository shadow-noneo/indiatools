Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between an experience certificate and a relieving letter? ▼
An Experience Certificate confirms the employee's role, duration of employment, and performance. A Relieving Letter formally releases the employee from their duties and obligations to the company. Most companies issue both documents simultaneously on the last working day.
Is an employer legally required to give an experience certificate? ▼
While there is no specific central law mandating experience certificates, withholding them can be considered harassment and may be actionable under labour laws. Most states have shop and establishment acts that require employers to issue service certificates. Employers who refuse can face legal consequences.
What if my previous employer refuses to give a relieving letter? ▼
If your employer refuses, you can: (1) Send a formal written request via email with acknowledgement, (2) Send a legal notice through our Legal Notice Generator, (3) File a complaint with the Labour Commissioner, (4) Approach the Labour Court. Most disputes are resolved at step 1 or 2.
Can I use this for a salary certificate for a bank loan? ▼
Yes! Select "Salary Certificate" type. This generates a document confirming the employee's current salary, which is accepted by most banks for personal loans, home loans, and credit card applications. Some banks may additionally require 3 months salary slips and Form 16.
