💡 Smart Loan Tips

Save lakhs on your loan with these expert tips

📉
Higher Down Payment
Paying 20-30% upfront reduces your principal dramatically. Even ₹2 lakh extra down payment saves ₹5-8 lakh in interest over 20 years.
🔄
Prepay When Possible
Making one extra EMI payment per year can cut 3-4 years off a 20-year loan. Most banks allow prepayment without penalty after 6 months.
🏦
Compare Banks
Even 0.5% difference in rate saves ₹1-2 lakh on a ₹50L loan. Always compare SBI, HDFC, ICICI, and Axis before finalising.
📊
Shorter Tenure
A 15-year loan vs 20-year loan on ₹50L saves over ₹8 lakh in interest, though your monthly EMI increases by ₹4,000-5,000.
📋
Good CIBIL Score
A CIBIL score above 750 qualifies you for the lowest interest rates. Check your score free at CIBIL.com before applying.
💡
Tax Benefits
Home loan principal (Section 80C, up to ₹1.5L) and interest (Section 24B, up to ₹2L) are tax deductible. Effective cost is lower!

Frequently Asked Questions

What is EMI? +
EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed amount you pay every month to repay your loan. It includes both the principal (original loan) and the interest charged by the bank. The formula is: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1), where P is principal, r is monthly interest rate, and n is number of months.
What is the current home loan interest rate in India? +
As of 2024, home loan interest rates in India typically range from 8.35% to 9.5% per annum depending on the bank. SBI offers rates starting at 8.5%, HDFC at 8.7%, ICICI at 8.75%, and Axis Bank at 8.75%. Rates depend on your CIBIL score, loan amount, and tenure.
How can I reduce my EMI? +
1) Increase your down payment to reduce the principal. 2) Choose a longer tenure (EMI reduces but total interest increases). 3) Negotiate a lower interest rate — a good CIBIL score (750+) helps. 4) Transfer your loan to a bank with lower rates (balance transfer). 5) Make partial prepayments whenever you have surplus money.
Is it better to take a loan for longer tenure? +
Longer tenure means lower monthly EMI but much higher total interest paid. For example, on a ₹50L home loan at 8.5%: 15 years = EMI ₹49,300, total interest ₹38.8L. 25 years = EMI ₹40,100, total interest ₹70.2L. You pay ₹31.4 lakh extra for the convenience of a lower monthly EMI.
What is a good EMI to salary ratio? +
Banks typically approve loans where total EMIs (all loans combined) are below 50% of your monthly take-home salary. Ideally, your home loan EMI alone should not exceed 30-35% of your monthly income. For example, if you earn ₹80,000/month, your home loan EMI should ideally stay below ₹28,000.
Can I prepay my loan early? +
Yes! For floating rate home loans, RBI guidelines prohibit banks from charging prepayment penalties. For fixed rate loans, banks may charge 2-3% of outstanding principal as prepayment fee. Prepaying even ₹1-2 lakh in year 3-5 of a home loan can save ₹4-6 lakh in total interest.
